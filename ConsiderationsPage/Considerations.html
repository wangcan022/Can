In general, using recycled water from a water tank is better than tap water and building a dam because of the following factors:

Advantages

  Money Saving Collect rain water for gardening and drinking water for livestock and lower overall bills.  Matured technology Water tanks today are built with formulated UV-resistant materials to provide taint-free, rust-free, hygienic, quality-controlled and cool water supply.  Easy to install and long durability Most of the water tanks providers offer a minimum 20 to 25 years warranty.  Livestock water trough Connect tanks with a livestock (solar) water trough so that water level always remain constant and warm during winter, and the ball valves are usually designed to prevent damage caused by livestock.

Disadvantages

 Regular Maintenance Dust or decayed materials may remain inside the water tank and weekly or monthly cleaning of the filter and the tanks are suggested.  Hidden cost Overhead, maintenance and installation costs for parts like first-flush filter and leaf-eater.  Reliability Issue Rainfall could be unpredictable and water in the tanks could be run out.  Storing Limits Size of tanks may be restricted depends on materials used. One tanks may not be sufficient but multiple tanks are costly.

Conclusion

Advantages of implementing a water tank outweigh its disadvantages. Rain water is better than tap water because tap water is precious and costly, while rain water is free and environment friendly. Moreover, water tanks may be better than dams (excavated tanks) for hobby farmers because of the scale and space needed. Dams are suitable for large scale agriculture in rural area. The construction of a hillside or sloping dam lead to excessive cost, and a dam with poor design could lead to downstream erosion and risks to property and people.
